Checkout Redesign

This one started with an audit, not a brief. Looking at the conversion data and the flow itself, it was obvious something was wrong.

Customers were losing revenue. The checkout had grown into a seven-step process: pages, modals, states, because product decisions had been made asynchronously over time, without anyone stepping back to look at the whole thing.

More than 30% of users were dropping off before completing an order.

More than 30% of users were dropping off before completing an order

I mapped every entry point into checkout: new order from the menu, order from a promo banner, returning to a saved order, abandoned cart emails.

I grouped what was similar, negotiated with product to remove what was unnecessary, and ran a competitive audit to understand the patterns users already know from market leaders.

Then I had to reconcile all of that with Square's own online ordering patterns, because GoParrot's checkout was built before the acquisition, so the two systems had never been properly aligned.

The hardest part was bridging GoParrot and Square patterns both on the UX and UI side

The hardest part was bridging GoParrot and Square patterns both on the UX and UI side. This happened during a design system migration, so I wasn't just redesigning flows. I was also creating additional components that didn't yet exist in Square's design system.

We got the flow down from seven steps to two. We also added guest checkout, which hadn't existed in the product before — something the competitive audit made clearly overdue.

The results came quickly: conversion up nearly 5%, abandoned cart rate down 17%, time to complete checkout down by 22 seconds. Users left positive feedback. The team picked it up from there and kept iterating.
