Case

Royalty Fee Collection

Franchise owners had been asking for this for a while — they needed a way to calculate and collect royalties from their franchisees directly inside Square.

Manual invoices, spreadsheets, third-party tools. We didn't want to send them elsewhere for something this core to running a franchise.

Sellers needed a way to calculate and collect royalties directly inside franchise dashboard

We ran customer interviews, synthesized all related requests. What became clear quickly: there's no single universal royalty formula, but there's one that covers most cases with small variations.

Some franchisors also wanted to automate everything, others send invoices manually. That shaped how flexible the system needed to be.

What became clear quickly: there's no single universal royalty formula

Before solving the UX, we had to solve the platform question. Franchise Suite lived as a third-party product relative to Square Dashboard, which meant the core flow could live in several different places.

I designed and explored three options: a native Franchise Suite experience, a Square Dashboard-native experience, and a hybrid. Each had different trade-offs in technical lift, time, and user experience continuity.

Each solution had different trade-offs in technical lift, time, and user experience continuity

We landed on a pragmatic hybrid: royalty configuration inside Franchise Suite, collection powered by Square's existing invoice infrastructure.

When additional development capacity wasn't available on the Square invoicing side, we scoped to a lightweight version covering the most common royalty model and delivering core value without the full feature set.

Users responded well, adoption rate hit 26% in first weeks. We iterated from there, adding things like automated deductions based on their feedback.

More importantly, it unblocked strategic deals. Royalty collection was a deal-breaker category for enterprise prospects — having it meant we could close accounts we couldn't before.
