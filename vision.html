Enterprise F&B Vision

After Square acquired GoParrot, the product didn't have a clear home. We spent the first year on integration, but there was no long-term vision: each team was pulling in its own direction, and enterprise clients were asking for things the product couldn't deliver. Competitors like Toast and Olo were already ahead on enterprise. In 2024, leadership finally gave us the green light to fix that.

After Square acquired GoParrot, the product lacked a strategic direction

Before touching Figma, I spent weeks gathering everything the company already knew — market research, customer interviews, sales feedback, roadmaps from five different teams, a product audit, competitive analysis. The real shift came when I stopped framing it as "what features are we missing" and started asking "why does complexity break for these clients at scale?"

That reframe changed everything. The problem wasn't features, it was the absence of scalability patterns in the product's architecture. A restaurant with one location has linear relationships inside its system. At 20 or 500 locations, complexity grows non-linearly. Without scalability patterns baked in, every new feature would break at enterprise scale.

The problem wasn't features, it was the absence of scalability patterns

That reframe changed everything. From there I had to solve three things in parallel:

  1. Design a realistic migration path from GoParrot into native Square
  2. Align the roadmaps and interests of several product teams
  3. Make sure the vision wasn't a designer artifact — but something that could actually be sliced into a product roadmap

The final deliverable was a leadership presentation covering future product behavior, a design pattern system for scaling, a phased transition plan, and a cross-team roadmap. The Head of Product, Design leadership and Sales team responded well. It helped unblock several stalled projects and gave a clear direction for the team. Location groups got funded and launched.
