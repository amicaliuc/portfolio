After Square acquired GoParrot (Franchise Suite), the product lacked a strategic direction. I led a vision initiative that gave the platform a north star, defining what Franchise Suite should become for enterprise F&B sellers, and how to get there.

Challenge

After Square acquired GoParrot, the product didn't have a clear home. We spent the first year on integration, but there was no long-term vision: each team was pulling in its own direction, and enterprise clients were asking for things the product couldn't deliver.

Competitors like Toast and Olo were already ahead on enterprise. Leadership needed a clear answer: what are we building, for whom, and in what order?

Leadership needed a clear answer: what are we building, for whom, and in what order?

Solution

Before touching Figma, I spent weeks gathering everything the company already knew: market research, customer interviews, sales feedback, roadmaps from five different teams, a product audit, competitive analysis.

The real shift came when I stopped framing it as 'what features are we missing' and started asking 'why does complexity break for these clients at scale?'

That reframe changed everything. The problem wasn't features, it was the absence of scalability patterns in the product's architecture.

A restaurant with one location has linear relationships inside its system. At 20+ locations, complexity grows non-linearly. Without scalability patterns baked in, every new feature would break at enterprise scale.

The problem wasn't features, it was the absence of scalability patterns

Impact

➀ The vision helped unblock and get funding for several stalled initiatives ➁ Location groups received funding and launched ➂ Enterprise menus moved forward ➃ Four teams gained a shared language and direction.

The final deliverable was a leadership presentation covering future product behavior, a design pattern system for scaling, a phased transition plan, and a cross-team roadmap.

The vision helped unblock several stalled multi-million dollar initiatives and align several product teams
